Android Pay
Redesigning Visitor On-site Experience

User Researcher

Team Size: 5      Date: Since 2017/1

User ResearchUsability TestingSurvey Design Heuristic EvaluationClass Project

Intro

Android Pay is a mobile payment app using Near-Field Communication (NFC) to make payment experiences in stores quicker and more efficient. Currently, Android Pay is competing directly with Apple Pay and Samsung Pay as they try to capture the nascent mobile-/NFC-payment market.

The market is growing very quickly, but as of yet the main early adopters are mostly male “technophiles”. Our client came to us with the statement that any research finding could be valuable but with an interest in learning about how to get more people onboard. So we scoped the problem down to focus on University Students, which is the population that we have most access to.

Goal

Our study sought to answer the question of what would motivate college students to use Android Pay, either for the first time, or more than they already do.

Approach

During the process, I want to try and incorporate User-Centered Design and Participatory Design, so that in all stages and for all decisions, the end users are involved. Thus, every iteration of design was tested with users.

Research

To better navigate the problem space we used the following tools to understand the complex problem at android_pay and ideate for solutions

Interaction Map: Learning the existing system

We used interaction map to better understand how to navigate the current system, and what could potentially be a usability issue.

We focused on three process in particular, which is first time use, general use, and instore payment

Contextual Interviews: Learning the context of problem

We took turn interviewing the employee at their work place to gain insights of their job and how it might influence the museum as a whole. Most interviews were conducted at their work space so that we can derive interview question onsite.

Affinity Wall: Understanding, intepreting, ideating

We use affinity wall to make sense of all the data we gathered through interview. Small intepretation sessions were hold within an 24 hours after the interview. After all interviews were done we also build a synthesized affinity wall to put identify problems expressed by multiple interviewees

Observation: See how people behave under natural setting

We spent time sitting at the museum lobby to observe visitor behavior and take notes on their actions. I also invited friends who have never visited the museum to explore the museum and followed them to take notes of how they navigate

Secondary Research: Support findings with studies

Each team member chose an area related to our client’s problem and did background research and literature review on it. Mine focused on psychographic of museum visitors, visitor expectations, case study on good practices.

Findings

Navigation is a big issue to current visitors.

Through affinity wall, we found out that many interviewees expressed frustration about the museum's current map and signage system.

"The map is crap"
Communication Manager
"One of the most common question at the store is where is the restroom"
Store Attendant
"We are trying to change the signage system, they are inconsistent throughout the museum"
Visitor Experience Manager

Which leads us to host observation session by inviting friends that never visited the museum to navigate it. During the session, I noticed that my friends and other visitors all had problems getting to the 2nd floor of the building and finding artworks that attracted them to visit the museum.

"Where can I find the Monet painting you mentioned?"
Friend I brought to the Museum for observation

After analyzing the data from interviews and observation session, we found out that these are the reasons why android_pay is hard to navigate for first time visitors.

android_pay is not effectively communicating their offerings.

During interviews with employees, we noticed that one major concern for android_pay is that lots of students don't even know its existence. Visitors also lack general information about the museum, such as what artwork it has, what events there are, or even the gallery hours.

The most prominent problem is that android_pay over relied on website for orienting their visitor and neglected on-site materials, even though lots of visitor don't even go to their website before visiting. For example, android_pay offers free tours on Sunday, however, the only way to know it onsite is a sign placed at the information desk only on Sunday.

Meanwhile, the website is also not executed in a visitor friendly way.

Other Findings

Most department lack effective way to collect visitor feedback.

Curators are unsure of the implementation in technology for android_pay.

Solutions

Brainstorm - Asking HMWs

We tried to look at our findings and rephrase the problem into questions of how might we.

Finding Examples - Visualizing is better than explaining

Then we tried to find examples of possible solutions to help our client better visualize the value.
