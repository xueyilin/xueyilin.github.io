Intro

    Background

  • 360 Security App is one of the company's flagship product with over 500 million users, it aims to protect Chinese android users from threats of malware and constant spam calls.

    • Why it is popular?

  • Chinese android phone users have no official place to download apps because government has blocked Google Services, thus easier to catch virus.

  • Mobile payment is a common thing is China, thus most people have important payment information in their phone, which lead to bigger concerns to mobile security.

  • Lots of spam call and texts incoming that interferes with their daily communication.

  • Lots of apps connecting constantly to their server costing huge data usage, but the telecom company only provides limited data plan, making people even more concerned about the data usage.

    • Problem

  • By 2014, 360 Security App's growth has slowed down due to increase of competition and out-dated interface.

    • Goal

  • The company want to re-vamp this product, keep it at the top of the market, and redesign it to reach out to a broader audience.

    • My Role

  • My team of 3 associate product manager was chosen to compete against three other product teams to redesign the product for its 6.0 version after an initial pitch to the CEO.

  • I worked on the user feedback analysis part of research and produced the wireframes for our design after brainstorming as a team.

Research

    We discover user needs through qualitative analysis

  • Our team mostly focused on the current user base, and did qualitative and quantitive analysis on user feedbacks.

  • We aggregate all user feedbacks in the recent three weeks into an excel worksheet and categorize them base on key terms, to locate the liked, wished, and longed for.

    • We identify current problem through competitive analysis.

  • Then we researched on five key competitors based on their features, information architecture, design, and unique selling point.

  • We investigated on their product forums, fan groups, and other relevant social media for feedbacks from users to better identify their users' point of view.

    • Surveys and interviews helped us narrow down the concepts.

  • We then worked with the user research team to better understand users' context.

  • 200+ Surveys, 8 interviews, and 3 focus groups were done to help us truly understand our users.

Findings

    Teens and women are two reach market we need to tap into.

  • The majority of our current users are young male adults from age 19 to 34.

  • Through interviews with teens and women, we found out that because 360 Security is too tool like and function oriented, they are not interested in trying.

    • Current users need familiarity, simplicity, and personality

  • Through the feedback analysis, we found out that the majority of users required simplicity and features that allow them to choose customized theme for the app.

  • Request to revert back to the 4.0 version interface that adopt Windows Phone's tile like structure was also lots of users'request.

    • 360 Security is packed with features that are too technical.

  • Through competitive analysis, we also realized that as compared to its competitors, 360 Security is not easy to use and is too packed due to its wide variety of sub-features.

  • Compared to its rising competitor Tecent Mobile Security, 360 Security is offering too many functions that are packaged under names that doesn't accurately present its value to users.

    • The information architecture and interaction design lack coherence.

  • All the pages for different features also varies, making users hard to learn, adapt, and build up a mental model.

  • Some sub-feature page has better information visualization, while others lack meaning.

Design

    Leaving just the essentials, transform subfeatures to downloadable plug-ins.

  • The first thing that we did is reorganizing the information architecture and to identify what is the most needed features.

  • Combined with findings from research, we decided to leave just the six most fundamental components: spam blocker, virus scanner, speed booster, data monitor, payment protector, and app manager.

    • Put actions at front and emotionalize the status of phone.

  • By investigating on the 4.0 version, which was widely well accepted by users, we decided to transfer what users like, which is status and information presenting in tiles right upon entrance to our design.

  • For example, if there were virus detected, the virus detector icon will become a warning sign with message below informing the dangerous level.

  • With an effort to make it more personal and appeal to teens and women, we also used words such as 'Great! You're Awesome'instead of scores to convey the phone status. We want our users to see it as a personal assistant instead of a tool.

    • Unify the features page with more visualization.

  • For features page, we try to unify the design, and adopt similar concepts of putting information upfront like the home page

  • We added more visualization to help users better interpret what to do.

    • Final Design

  • Our final design was highly praised by the CEO, and there was discussion of making it a younger verison that targets students.

Impact

  • The current live version also applied lots of our ideas, including: the notification widget, putting information upfront, and modulizing addition features for download.

  • After this launch, 360 Security's user base has increased by 24.6%.
